Literature review
Contemporary stage of medicine’s development is characterized by rapid adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) systems, which are used to solve tasks, traditionally requiring human intelligence (Alowais et al., 2023). AI includes different methods, such as machine learning, deep learning and natural language processing (Alowais et al., 2023). Despite AI’s potential in revolutionizing health care, effective diagnostic remains a global issue due to the complexity of mechanisms of diseases (Alowais et al., 2023). For countries with middle income level, including Kazakhstan, AI is considered as a strategical instrument for strengthening national health care systems (Ciecierski-Holmes et al., 2022). Existing literature highlights that the success of adopting such technologies is directly related to how future specialists percept these systems during their learning process (Alowais et al., 2023). In Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) the central place is occupied by studying Explainable AI (XAI) as a means of overcoming the transparency barrier (Markus et al., 2021). This review systematizes theoretical approaches to the explanation design and analyzes factors defining medical students’ readiness to adopt these tools. The existing literature indicates that for Kazakhstan, as for middle-income country, the adaptation of global XAI practices is a prerequisite for the digitalization of medicine (Ciecierski-Holmes et al., 2022).
Theoretical foundations and taxonomy of explainability. In the field of HCI, it is fundamental to distinguish between the concepts of interpretability (human understanding of the process) and fidelity (the degree to which an explanation corresponds to the actual logic of an algorithm) (Markus et al., 2021). The manifesto "XAI 2.0" asserts that current challenges require moving away from purely technical metrics towards an interdisciplinary dialogue that considers the psychological aspects of perception (Longo et al., 2024). human perception of AI is based on the dual process models, suggesting switch between the fast intuitive “System 1” and the slow analytical “System 2” (Wang et al., 2019). Effective design of XAI should minimize cognitive distortions of System 1 (such as anchoring bias or confirmation bias) (Wang et al., 2019). Contemporary taxonomy classifies explanation methods as attribution-based (visualization of the contribution of features), model-based (rules) and example-based (prototypes and counterfactuals) (Markus et al., 2021). Research in cognitive science confirms that people naturally prefer contrastive explanations, answering on questions “Why A, but not B?” (Miller, 2019).
The crisis of trust and the role of verifiability. Literature reveals a paradox in which the availability of explanations often does not lead to an increase in the objective accuracy of human and AI collaboration (Vaccaro et al., 2024). Meta-analysis of 106 unique experiments demonstrates that coordination in human-AI pairs is often difficult, which prevents the achievement of synergy (Vaccaro et al., 2024). One of the reasons of this phenomenon is the overreliance risk (excessive trust), when a user accepts wrong advice of a system due to a convincing justification (Buçinca et al., 2021). Data shows that many standard XAI methods describe only the decision-making process (process explanations), but do not help the user verify its correctness (Fok & Weld, 2024). In this regard, the concept of verifiability has been introduced into scientific discourse — the ability of an explanation to allow a person to quickly determine the correctness of an algorithm (Fok & Weld, 2024). This ability is critical for achieving complementary performance, when a team’s productivity exceeds the capabilities of each participant individually (Fok & Weld, 2024).
Advantages of the Evaluative AI paradigm. The evaluative AI paradigm seems to be a more effective method of building informed trust among medical students compared to traditional recommendation systems (Miller, 2023). The rationale is that the traditional recommend-and-defend approach deprives the user of autonomy by imposing a ready-made answer, while evaluative AI provides evidence “for” and “against” a person's own hypotheses (Miller, 2023). This model supports option awareness and forces users to critically evaluate compromises between different options (Miller, 2023). Empirical data in the diagnostics field confirms that hypothesis-oriented approach sufficiently improves the quality of a doctor's decision-making (Miller, 2023). 
The importance of Cognitive Forcing functions. The cognitive forcing functions (CFF) inclusion in explanation interfaces is necessary to prevent automated bias among novice specialists (Buçinca et al., 2021). The reason is that simplified and convenient explanations provoke passive acceptance of information and reduce the analytical activity of the brain (Buçinca et al., 2021). CFF make a subject use "System 2", requiring them to formulate their own opinion before becoming familiar with the position of AI (Buçinca et al., 2021). Empirical tests confirm significant decrease of overreliance level on erroneous predictions when using CFF methods (Buçinca et al., 2021). The use of partial explanations is also considered as a method of activating cognitive coercion, forcing the user to fill in gaps in logic on their own (de Jong et al., 2025).
The effectiveness of contrastive formats in learning. Contrastive explanations predicting typical human errors (predicted foils) are the most promising type of XAI for improving students' long-term readiness to work with AI (Buçinca et al., 2025). This is because such formats aim to address specific knowledge gaps by explaining the reasons for the fallacy of alternatives (Buçinca et al., 2025). In a large-scale experiment on the Prolific platform (N=628), it was recorded that the group with contrastive predicted explanations showed a higher level of learning (M = 0.47) compared to the group with one-sided explanations (M = 0.39) (Buçinca et al., 2025). The effectiveness is also evidenced by the fact that the participants retained their independent decision-making skills even after disabling AI support (Buçinca et al., 2025). The method of learning through comparison directly correlates with the tasks of differential diagnosis, where it is critically important to understand the reasons for excluding alternatives (Buçinca et al., 2025). Thus, the contrasting format can serve as a tool for students' professional development, building trust in the system as a competent mentor (Buçinca et al., 2025).
Moderating factors and regional specifics. The perception of AI systems is significantly moderated by the level of preliminary user expertise (Wang & Yin, 2021). Standard methods for visualizing the importance of features (feature importance) often turn out to be useless for undergraduates due to the lack of the necessary context for interpretation (Wang & Yin, 2022). For beginners, explanations can create an "illusion of explanatory depth", causing false confidence in the system (Wang & Yin, 2022). Research in Low Middle-Income Countries (LMIC) countries highlights that trust is undermined by the lack of local context in the training data, which requires XAI to be adapted to regional protocols (Ciecierski-Holmes et al., 2022). The integration of AI literacy training into educational programs is recognized as a prerequisite for the formation of proper adaptation of doctors to new technologies (Alowais et al., 2023).
Conclusion. An analysis of the scientific literature indicates the need to move from simple recommendation models to evaluative AI assessment formats to ensure the reasonable trust of medical students (Miller, 2023). The use of contrastive explanations is considered as an effective method of improving the quality of decision-making by focusing on specific gaps in user knowledge (Buçinca et al., 2025). Today, in the context of LMIC, there is a shortage of regional studies comparing these paradigms in real clinical practice (Ciecierski-Holmes et al., 2022). Further empirical testing of the impact of various types of explanations on the willingness to implement AI is recognized as a necessary step for the successful integration of technology into medical education and national health standards (Alowais et al., 2023).
